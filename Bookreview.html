About the Book

Trevor Noah's unlikely path from apartheid South Africa began with a criminal act:his birth. Trevor was born to a white swiss father and a black Xhosa mother at a time when such a union was punishable by five years in prison.

Living proof of his parents' indiscreation, Trevor was kept mostly indoors for the earliest years of his life,bound by the extreme and often absurb measures his mother took to hide him from the government that could ,at any moment,steal him away.

Finally liberated by the end of S.A's tyrannical white rule,Trevor and his mother set on a grand adventure,living openly and freely and embracing the opportunities won by a centuries-long struggle.

Born a crime is basically a story of a mischievous young boy who grows into a a restless young man as he struggles to find himself in a world where he was never supposed to exit.

It is also the story of that young man's relationship with his fearless,rebellious and religious mother-his teammate,a woman dtermined to save her son from the cycle of poverty,violence and abuse that would ultimately threaten her own life.

The stories collected here are by turned hilarious,dramatic and deeply affecting. Whether subsiting on caterpillars for dinner during hard times,being thrown from a movinng car during an attempted kidnapping or just trying to survive the life and death pitfalls of dating in highschool,Trevor illiminates his curious world with an incisive wit and unflinching honesty./

His stories weave together to form a moving funny portrait of a boy making his way through a damaged world in a dangerous time,armed only with a keen sense of humour and a mother's unconvectional, unconditional love.
